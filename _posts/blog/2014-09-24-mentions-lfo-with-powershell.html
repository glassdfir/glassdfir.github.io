In the DFIR world, it can be important to understand the principle of Least Frequency of Occurrence (LFO). The first place I read about this was on Harlan Carvey's BlogÂ a few years ago. The general concept is that your system processes and favorite applications should be the most abundant items in your process logs. Inversely, malware should be the least abundant by comparison as most are designed to not draw a lot of attention. Sometimes you find yourself without much of a pivot point and you are asked to "find evil".

With that in mind, here is an example of how you can use Sysmon to list all the processes, sorted by frequency count:
